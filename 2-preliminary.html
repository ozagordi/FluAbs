Comment

There is an effect of vaccinations pretty much everywhere. Exceptions are,
for the IC50:

  • H3 Brisbane (small effect),
  • H4.

For the EC50 they are:

  • H12,
  • H3,
  • H4,
  • H7 virus,
  • H7.

On the other hand we already knew it, since flu shots increase with age.

library(ggplot2)
library(MASS)
p <- ggplot(data = ic_data, aes(x = age, y = flushot_avg)) + geom_jitter()
p <- p + geom_smooth(method = "loess")
p

Do titers increase with the shots alone? This is more difficult to see from a plot only. For example, the smoothing below shows a moderate increase of H2 titer for a range of shuts between zero and ten.

p <- ggplot(data = ic_data, aes(x = flushot_avg, H2_log)) + geom_jitter()
p <- p + geom_smooth(method = "loess")
p

In my opinion, the correct way to discern the effect of age and vaccinations is to perform a regression with both terms and compare it to a regression with the age only. We do it for H2, IC50 data.
